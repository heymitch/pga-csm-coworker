# Assignment Review Playbook

## Module-Specific Rubrics

### Module 0: Crash Course Takeaways
- **What they submit:** Takeaways from Module 0 video
- **What to check:** Did they identify specific, actionable insights?
- **Feedback approach:** Comment on one specific takeaway, encourage their understanding
- **Tone:** Warm, welcoming — this is their first interaction

### Module 1: Bio as a Premium Ghostwriter
- **What they submit:** "I ghostwrite Educational Email Courses for..."
- **What to check:**
  - Niche specificity — NOT just "Health & Wellness" or "CEOs" or "Creators"
  - Credibility statement (interest, experience, training, life changed, hours studied)
  - If multiple services listed, is ghostwriting the lead?
- **Common issues:**
  - Too broad (industry only, role only, unclear business type)
  - No credibility section
  - Using "expert" instead of "coach," "consultant," etc.
- **Coaching move:** Have them list top 30-50 ideal clients and find commonalities
- **Resources:** Bonus Positioning Q&A (Module 1), Better Bio Checklist in Niche FAQs

### Module 2: EEC Outline
- **What they submit:** Title + Headline + Whisper + 5 Mistakes + Outcomes
- **What to check:**
  - Title calls out audience + has asset type (Blueprint, Guide, etc.)
  - Headline is specific and tangible — could only work for THIS EEC
  - Whisper overcomes objection or establishes authority (not just "And How To Avoid Them")
  - Mistakes are NOT all "Nots" (Failing to, Neglecting, Ignoring, Overlooking) — call out wrong actions they ARE doing
  - Negative outcomes are tangible and niche-specific (not generic: "Burnout," "Frustration," "Leaving Money On The Table")
  - Audience called out throughout, not just in the title
- **Give max 2-3 tips** — don't overwhelm
- **Resources:** Elite Outline Checklist in Niche FAQs, Module 3 for titles

### Module 3: Writing the 5-Day EEC
- **What they submit:** Full EEC (Notion, Google Doc, or pasted)
- **What to check:**
  - NOT a direct ChatGPT copy — flag "delve into," "embark on a journey," "unlock the power"
  - Personality and voice present — personal examples, stories, slang
  - Each day 800-1200 words (400-600 too short, 1550+ too long)
  - Formatting: Expanders as subheaders, varied bullets/numbers, NOT "novel-like" long paragraphs, NOT "longer outline" with no prose
  - Speaking to niche audience's specific challenges/desires/goals
  - Cole's 1:3:1 structure used (short opener, 3-sentence description, summary)
  - Day 0 doesn't give away all 5 mistakes explicitly — tease without revealing
- **Feedback template:** Lead with what's strong, then 2 tips (personality/voice + formatting or specificity)
- **Resources:** Exceptional EEC Checklist in Niche FAQs

### Module 4: Landing Page
- **What to check:**
  - Page 1 above the scroll (CTA button visible without scrolling)
  - Left/right columns match in length (2-column template)
  - Title larger font + different color from headline
  - Niche called out immediately — reader self-identifies
  - Specific outcomes in headline
  - Graphic/image included and "productized"
  - CTA button: 3-5 words, bright color
  - Below-scroll days DON'T give away the 5 mistakes — explain importance without revealing
  - No orphan words (single word on its own line)
  - Same word not repeated 3+ times in headline
- **Resources:** Module 4 Landing Page Checklist in Skool

### Module 5: Contact Dashboard
- **What they submit:** 50+ names on Contact Dashboard + Leaks/Faucets count + Primary Platform in #submit-assignment-5
- **What to check:**
  - Dashboard has 50+ names
  - Balance of Leaks (direct prospects) and Faucets (connectors)
  - Primary outreach platform identified
  - Started any outreach yet?
- **Feedback:** Encourage + ask about their Outreach Schedule (25/week goal)
- **If lacking:** Ask how they can dig deeper to add more contacts

### Module 6: Free Consulting Loom
- **What to check:**
  - Under 5 minutes
  - Opens with positive comment about prospect's business
  - Identifies a PROBLEM (not just pitching the solution)
  - Shows problem → agitates → then presents solution (EEC)
  - Screen shares prospect's website/socials
- **Key feedback:** "Start with the problem, not the solution"

### Module 7: Sales Call
- **What they submit:** Recording or self-reflection
- **What to check:**
  - If recording: identify 1 key improvement area (don't critique everything)
  - If self-reflection: discuss what went well and what to improve
  - Follow-up plan: are they following up with this prospect?
  - Ongoing outreach: not putting all eggs in one basket?

### Module 8-9: Landed & Fulfilled / Advanced
- **Feedback approach:** Celebration + next steps toward Liftoff readiness

## Feedback Format

```
[Positive opener — specific to their work]

[Tip 1 — most impactful improvement]

[Tip 2 — secondary improvement (optional)]

[Curriculum link — specific Module/FAQ/Checklist]

[Encouragement closer — "Onwards!" or progress-appropriate]
```

## AI Detection Checklist

Flag if you see these phrases (likely ChatGPT output):
- "Embark on a journey/odyssey"
- "Unlock the power"
- "Delve into"
- "In today's fast-paced world"
- "It's not just about X, it's about Y"
- Ladder-looking formatting (all bullets, no prose)
